## 1. Getting Started & Profile Creation
When you launch the app, you will be prompted to create or select a **User Profile**. 
- **Isolated Databases**: Each profile maintains its own database, settings, chat history, and uploaded papers.
- **Switching Profiles**: You can switch profiles at any time from the bottom-left profile dropdown in the main sidebar.

---

## 2. Managing Projects
All research activities are organized inside **Projects** (e.g., "Machine Learning", "Climate Change").
- **Active Project**: Visible in the top-left sidebar indicator.
- **Creating a Project**: Navigate to the **Research Library** page and click the **New Project** button at the top.
- **Deleting a Project**: Click **Delete Project** to remove the project workspace. *Warning: This permanently deletes all associated papers, FAISS indexes, and chat histories under this project.*

---

## 3. Uploading Research Papers
To upload academic papers (PDF format) to your active project:
1. Navigate to the **Upload Papers** page on the sidebar.
2. Click **Browse Files** to open a file dialog, or drag-and-drop one or more PDF files directly onto the dashed drag area.
3. The files will enter the **Indexing Queue** at the bottom:
   - **Text Extraction**: The app extracts page text. If a page contains images (scanned PDFs), it will fallback to Tesseract OCR (if installed).
   - **Metadata Extraction**: A local/cloud LLM parser analyzes the document to automatically extract the Title, Authors, Abstract, Keywords, DOI, and Publication Year.
   - **Vector Indexing**: The text is split into overlapping chunks, vectorized using your selected embedding model (`sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2`), and saved in your local FAISS database.
   - **Duplicate Protection**: If the app detects a duplicate by file hash, DOI, or title, a popup will ask if you want to overwrite the existing paper.

## 4. Using the Research Library
The **Research Library** is your central document explorer.
- **Papers Table**: View all uploaded papers. Double-click any row, or click **Open PDF** in the action column, to open the PDF file in your system's default PDF viewer.
- **Search Options**:
   - **Text Filter**: Type keywords into the search bar to filter papers by title, author, or metadata.
   - **Semantic Vector Search**: Check **Semantic Vector Search** and type a concept or question (e.g., "unsupervised learning techniques"). The app will query the FAISS index and list the most semantically relevant text passages across all papers in the active project.
- **Paper Details & Insights**: Selecting a paper in the table updates the right-hand panel:
   - **Executive Summary & Contributions**: View the structured summary generated by the LLM (Abstract, Objective, Methodology, Results, Conclusions, and Limitations).
   - **Citations Bibliography**: Auto-generated **APA** and **BibTeX** bibliography strings ready to copy-paste into your manuscript.
   - **Action Buttons**: **Copy Citation** to copy the APA reference, or **Delete Paper** to permanently purge it from SQLite and the FAISS index.
- **Smart Collections**: Click **New Smart Collection** to group papers based on rules (e.g., "Published after 2024" or "Contains the keyword 'Transformer'").

## 5. Research Chat & Specialized Modes
The **Research Chat** page lets you interact directly with your documents.
- **Chat Scope**: By default, the chat searches across all papers in the project. Click **Scope: X Target Papers** at the top to select specific papers for the assistant to reference.
- **Assistant Modes (Personas)**:
   - **General**: standard research assistant persona.
   - **Paper Reviewer**: acts as an academic peer reviewer, critiquing methodology and weaknesses.
   - **Literature Reviewer**: focuses on synthesizing concepts across multiple target papers.
   - **Thesis Coach**: guides academic writing, structuring, and argument formulations.
   - **Comparison Expert**: analyzes contrasts and similarities between target papers.
- **Source Attributions**: When the assistant answers, the right-hand **Source Attributions** panel will list the exact chunks, page numbers, and similarity scores used to generate the answer. Click on an attribution to view the snippet.
- **Export Transcript**: Click **Export Transcript** to export your complete chat history to a formatted PDF.

---

## 6. Comparing Research Papers
To compare multiple papers side-by-side:
1. Navigate to the **Paper Comparison** page on the sidebar.
2. Select the papers you want to compare from the checklist.
3. Click **Generate Comparison Matrix**.
4. The LLM will construct a side-by-side comparative table analyzing the chosen papers across key dimensions:
   - *Core Objective*
   - *Methodology / Architecture*
   - *Dataset / Target Subject*
   - *Key Results & Accuracies*
   - *Limitations & Open Challenges*
5. Click **Export Matrix** to save the comparative table as a PDF report or a CSV spreadsheet.

## 7. Research Memory & Knowledge Retention
The **Research Memory** page acts as the app's cognitive engine.
- **Facts Stored**: View key facts and conclusions extracted from your conversations.
- **Adding/Editing Memory**: You can manually register core definitions, custom annotations, or formulas that the LLM should memorize and prioritize during chats.

## 8. Settings, API Keys & Portable Mode
Navigate to **Settings** to customize the app's behavior:
- **API Key Management**: Set up connections and model choices for:
  - Google Gemini (e.g., `gemini-2.5-flash`, `gemini-1.5-pro`)
  - OpenAI (e.g., `gpt-4o`, `gpt-4o-mini`)
  - Anthropic Claude
  - Groq & OpenRouter
  - **Ollama**: Connect to local models (e.g., `llama3`, `mistral`) for 100% offline generation.
- **Vector Settings**: Select your default embedding model (defaults to `sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2`).
- **Data & Portability**:
  - **Data Location**: Displays where the database and uploads are stored on disk.
  - **Enable Portable Mode**: Saves all configurations and uploaded files inside the application root directory rather than `AppData`. Ideal for running the app directly from a USB flash drive or portable hard drive.

## 9. Troubleshooting & Support
- **Error binding parameter 8 (Type 'list' is not supported)**: Automatically resolved. This occurred when the LLM extracted multiple authors as a list instead of a string. Input types are now defensively normalized.
- **UI freezes during concurrent uploads**: Thread-safe locks (`threading.Lock` and `threading.RLock`) are active on both the SQLite database and the FAISS vector database. Concurrent operations will queue up safely without blocking the application.
- **Log Files**: If you encounter an unexpected error, look at the detailed log file at:
  `C:\Users\<Username>\AppData\Local\ResearchPaperAssistantPro\logs\app.log`
